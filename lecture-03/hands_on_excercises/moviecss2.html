The Godfather

The Godfather (1972) is a legendary crime drama about the powerful Corleone family and their Mafia empire. The film follows Michael Corleone (Al Pacino), who reluctantly becomes involved in his family's criminal world after his father, Vito Corleone (Marlon Brando), is attacked. Known for its iconic performances and themes of loyalty and power, it is considered one of the greatest films of all time.
